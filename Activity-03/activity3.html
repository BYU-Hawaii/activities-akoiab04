More than 10 percent of the school's graduates go on to higher education.
Moroni High's strong vocational program also helps more than 60 percent of its 388 students find jobs before they graduate.
It is extremely unusual in Kiribati - a country that empasizes academics - to find a school that provides vocationaly training.
The missionary couples help motivate the students. Their dedication is one factor in the school's success.
Moroni High is one of five schools in Tarawa, four are private schools sponsored by religious organizations, the fifth is a public school.
The LDS school covers Forms 1-6, which is equivalent to grades seven through 12. Classes are taught in English, the official language of the island nation.
All students are required to take religion, English, math, social studies and sciend classes.
The school also offers courses in office skills, computers, music, home economics, physical education and wood and metal shop.
